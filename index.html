An example of a lean and framework-less approach to modern frontend development.

With the increasing robustness and maturity of "the browser is the platform", our application-delivery ...

The flexibility of the web-stack, based on the HTML, CSS and JS primitives, can be abstracted as much as one like, over the last years its been increasingly rare to see web-applications developed without a framework, compile/build step and megabytes of node_modules, all seeking to improve developer experience - but resulting in ruining the maintainer experience. The software we develop for the web has never been more fragile. Have you tried to load up a web project from just a couple of years ago? On a new laptop? With a newer version of Node? Did it use the (binary) swc? Any deprecated packages? Any security warnings? Abandoned dependencies?

This project is about using as few dependencies and abstractions as possible - while still embracing the (arguably) biggest things in "web": design-systems, custom HTML elements and web-gl.

Design-Systems

Design has always been messy - and its almost impossible to ensure a clean, systematic, maintainable, documented and flexible solution to ui-design. Only a few companies have succeeded, either by massive investments outside the reach of most (99.9%) of client projects, or by a enthusiast, un-funded open source approach. Introducing System to the design dicipline, presents the argument that 'design' alone (no matter how effective, communicative and beautiful) is now not enough. We need a systematic approach - to implementation and delivery of the design. The role of 'design engineer' was born.
